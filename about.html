About the Exhibit

“The emergence of something that previously did not exist” is the meaning of the exhibit’s title: Poiesis. Now on its 15th year, the collaboration between students from the College of Fine Arts and the College of Music continues to grow and take new form. Together, this collaboration moves forward to create a harmonious exhibit that blends visuals and music.

Like the rings of a tree that hold traces of its past, Poiesis 15: Embarking Home is a journey through time, memory, and creation.

This exhibit is the culmination of an interdisciplinary collaboration between the University of the Philippines Diliman's College of Fine Arts and College of Music, developed in the first semester and unveiled in the second. At its heart is a simple, resonant theme: Home.

Each painting and haiku from Prof. Mitzi Marie Aguilar-Reyes’ FA 10 Visual Perception class, and animation from Prof. Annie Dennise Pacaña’s VC 26 class is shaped by personal interpretations of sound. These works were inspired by original musical compositions produced by the students of Prof. Jesher Edrei Perez and Prof. Joan Hope’s MuEd 100 class during the first semester.

However, Embarking Home is more than an act of translation. It is a reflection of creative process and passage. Each piece captures a moment of connection, shaped by the student’s unique experiences, memories, and interpretations.

Months later, these works are exhibited not as a beginning or an end, but as part of an unfolding journey. An embodiment of what it means to grow, to return, to carry something forward.

We invite you to embark on your own journey through these artworks. Look, feel, and remember that home, like art, like trees, like us: is always in the making.
